Siargao Island

Siargao, the surfing capital of the Philippines, is famous for the powerful Cloud 9 wave. Beyond the barrels, explore the tidal flat pools of Magpupungko, kayak through the lush mangroves, and relax in the serene, turquoise waters of Sugba Lagoon.

Boracay Island Activities

Experience the best of tropical luxury with a variety of activities including scuba diving, snorkeling trips, and sunset paraw sailing. D'Mall offers dining and shopping, culminating in vibrant beachside nightlife, making it the country's party hub.

Banaue Rice Terraces

Often called the "Eighth Wonder of the World," the Banaue Rice Terraces were hand-carved into the mountains of Ifugao over 2,000 years ago. These breathtaking terraces, still used for planting rice and vegetables, showcase the resilience and ingenuity of the indigenous Ifugao people.

Vigan City

Vigan is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the few Spanish colonial towns left in the Philippines. Take a horse-drawn carriage ride (kalesa) along the beautifully preserved cobblestone street of Calle Crisologo and savor authentic Ilocano cuisine and local historical marvels.

Mayon Volcano

Mayon Volcano, known for its perfect cone shape, is an active stratovolcano and a stunning landmark of the Bicol Region. Its surrounding landscape is ideal for hiking, ATV adventures across the lava trails, and viewing ancient ruins like the Cagsawa Bell Tower, which was partially submerged by a previous eruption.

Cebu & Moalboal

Cebu offers both city vibrancy and incredible natural experiences. Head to Moalboal for the incredible Sardine Run, where millions of sardines swim just offshore. For adrenaline seekers, canyoneering through the turquoise waters of the majestic Kawasan Falls is a must-do experience.

Coron, Palawan

Coron is famous for its dramatic limestone cliffs, seven stunning freshwater lakes, and world-class diving sites featuring Japanese shipwrecks from WWII. Key destinations include Kayangan Lake (often cited as the cleanest lake in Asia) and Barracuda Lake, both offering unique and breathtaking snorkeling opportunities.

Batanes: Northern Charm

Batanes is characterized by its dramatic seascapes, rolling hills, and unique Ivatan stone houses built to withstand strong winds and typhoons. Explore the traditional villages, watch the sunrise over the Pacific, and experience a unique, isolated culture in the northernmost frontier of the Philippines.
