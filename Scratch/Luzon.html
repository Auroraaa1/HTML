Explore the Wonders of Luzon

The Northern Jewel

Luzon, the largest island in the Philippines, is home to the nation's capital, Manila, and a vast array of historical sites, majestic mountains, and vibrant cultural landscapes. From the cool mountain city of Baguio to the ancient rice terraces of the Cordilleras, Luzon offers a diverse and enriching travel experience.

Must-Visit Attractions

Baguio City

Baguio is known as the Summer Capital of the Philippines. It is a city on a mountain, and the climate is cool all year. The city offers pine trees, gardens, and local markets. Visitors like Burnham Park and Mines View Park. The city stays busy but gives a chance to rest from the heat of the lowlands. The main roads are busy, but the smaller streets offer a peaceful walk.

Mayon Volcano

Mayon Volcano is known for its near perfect cone shape. It attracts visitors who want to see the volcano from safe viewing areas in Albay. The nearby towns offer short tours, ATV rides, and local food. The mountain changes with the weather, so every visit feels different. Many tourists take photos from the Cagsawa Ruins because the view is clear on good days.

El Nido, Palawan

El Nido is known for its clear water, tall limestone cliffs, and quiet islands. Many tourists visit for island hopping. The lagoons and beaches are easy to enjoy because the tours follow set routes. The town has small restaurants and simple places to stay. Visitors like the mix of nature and calm surroundings. The area is easy to explore with short boat rides.
