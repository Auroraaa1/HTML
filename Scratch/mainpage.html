Popular Foods in the Philippines

Adobo

The most popular Filipino food and referred to as the unofficial national dish of the Philippines, Adobo is commonly chicken simmered in vinegar, garlic, black peppercorns, soy sauce, and bay leaves. Though its an authentic Filipino delicacy, Adobo gets its name from colonizer Spain, which means “marinade” or “sauce” and is usually eaten with white rice

Sinigang

a popular Filipino sour and savory soup or stew, characterized by its sour broth that gets its distinct flavor from ingredients like tamarind, guava, or calamansi. It is a versatile dish that can be made with various proteins, including pork, beef, fish, or shrimp, and is filled with a mix of vegetables such as daikon radish, string beans, okra, and leafy greens.

Lechon

Lechon, derived from a Spanish word for roasted suckling pig is one of the most popular dishes in the Philippines. The slowly-roasted suckling pig is usually stuffed with lemongrass, tamarind, garlic, onions, and chives, and is then roasted on a large bamboo spit over an open fire. It is traditionally served whole on a platter, at celebrations and festive events such as weddings and Christmas. Once the meat is properly roasted and falls off the bone, people tend to eat every part of the pig, and the crispy, reddish-brown, crackling skin is especially beloved.
